Readability scores you can easily understand

The most common readability metrics can be counterintuitive. To improve the readability of your writing, you conversely want to lower the grade level of your text. This fact trips up many writers. They assume that if their work scores a low grade level, it must be written for children. This couldn't be further from the truth. Many novels written by authors, including Ernest Hemingway, are written at a 5th-grade level but intended for adults.

Another common mistake is overestimating the average reading level of your audience. To help with both of these issues, Hemingway provides both the grade level score and clear written feedback. If your text is too complicated, Hemingway will let you know that your readability score is poor and suggest a different target grade level.
